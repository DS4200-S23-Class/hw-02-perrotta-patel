Task Abstraction

    
    
        
            
                             
            

High-level task:

Consume -> Discover: As depicted by the visualization, viewiers can take a look at the temperature over time in Vancouver come across potential trends.
            

Mid-level task:

Search -> Lookup: The target is idenfied in red where the last 3 days are highlighted. The maximum temperatures for these days can be found by looking at these data points.
            

Low-level task:

Compare: There are multiple targets that can be compared amongst each other as well as with the rest of the data points.
            

Target:

All data -> Trends: Examining the trends of the maximum temperatures in Vancouver and seeing how the last 3 days fit in.
        
        
            
                             
            

High-level task:

Consume -> Present: Information is being displayed in a manner where users can understand without needing any supplemental annotation.
            

Mid-level task:

Search -> Browse: Visualization can answer the question of whether Seattle or Portland have more days with daily mean temperature 20 degrees above normal.
            

Low-level task:

Compare -> A comparison of temperature between the two cities can be made.
            

Target:

All Data: Features -> Identification of if there is a higher occurence of daily temperatures after a certain year or vice versa across the 2 cities.
